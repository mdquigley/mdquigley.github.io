Toneblocks

Summary: Masters thesis project developed under the study of Dr. Morwaread Farbood.

Tags: Web Audio API, Tone.js, Blockly, JavaScript, Usability Testing

Explanation: Toneblocks is a block-based visual programming environment for creating music with code. The development, documentation, and evaluation of the project served as my masters thesis at NYU Steinhardt. In examining the strengths and weaknesses of music making and computer programming educational tools, and the relatively empty space between them, I sought to generate a tool that is both intuitive and interesting for novice users with interests in computer programming and music. In designing such a tool, user-centered design principles were employed in an effort to lower barriers of entry while increasing the level at which advanced users became inhibited or uninterested. Influenced by educational theory as well as design theory, the web application integrates block-based visual programming with music making activities. An ongoing design-based research approach allowed for continual assessment with an industry expert and subsequent improvement to further address user-centered design principles. Observational usability testing sessions showed the application to be easy to use, as well as interesting in its musical affordances. Limitations revealed in assessment offer potential future improvements and additions.

The tool is in ongoing development, a live demo is available for use at Toneblocks
